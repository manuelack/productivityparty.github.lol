ü§ù Tie-Breaker Procedure:

In the event of a tie where two or more guests finish with the same highest point total, the following tie-breaker procedure will be implemented:

1. Inspection of Work:

Non-tied guests will review the work of those in the tie. This inspection is to ensure fairness and transparency in assessing the quality and quantity of work done.

2. Voting for the Winner:

After inspecting the work, the non-tied guest will vote to determine the winner. Each non-tied guest gets one vote.

3. Final Decision:

The guest among the tied individuals who receives the most votes from their peers will be declared the winner of the Productivity Party and awarded the $100 prize.

This tie-breaker process is designed to encourage not only productivity but also the quality of work, ensuring that the most deserving guest wins in the case of a tie.

üé© Host's Role

At the Productivity Party, a designated host plays a crucial role in managing the event. Here are the key responsibilities of the host:

- Timing: The host is responsible for keeping track of time, ensuring that all sessions and breaks start and end as per the schedule.

- Initiating Sessions: The host will officially start each work session and announce the beginning of breaks. Guests must follow these cues strictly.

- Monitoring Rules: The host will monitor guests to ensure adherence to the rules. This includes checking for punctuality, ensuring silence during work sessions, and observing break durations.

- Points Deduction: The host has the authority to deduct points for any rule violations. Their decisions on point deductions are final and must be respected by all guests.

- Handling Disputes: In case of any disputes or uncertainties about the rules, the host's decision will be considered final.

- Tie-Breaker Supervision: In the event of a tie, the host will oversee the inspection of work and the voting process, ensuring that it is carried out fairly and transparently.

The role of the host is to ensure that the Productivity Party runs smoothly and that the competition is fair and enjoyable for all guests. Their decisions are made in the best interest of maintaining the integrity and spirit of the event.
